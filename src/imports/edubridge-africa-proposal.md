Problem Statement

Many students and young learners across Africa face difficulties in accessing quality education and digital skills. Most learning platforms are expensive, require fast and constant internet connection, or are not designed for the African learning environment.

Because of limited resources and poor internet access, many learners struggle to study effectively and gain practical digital skills. There is a need for a simple, affordable, and well-structured learning platform that supports students and beginners across Africa.

Proposed Solution :

EduBridge Africa is a web-based educational platform designed to help students and beginners learn digital skills in a simple and practical way.

The platform will provide short and easy-to-understand lessons that do not require much internet data. Learners can study at their own pace using a clean and simple interface that works well on mobile devices. EduBridge Africa focuses on helping learners move from basic theory to practical skills while tracking their learning progress.

Key Objectives :
Improve access to quality learning resources
Support self-paced learning for beginners
Reduce the need for high-speed internet
Help learners stay motivated through progress tracking

Target Users :
Secondary school students
University and college students
Self-learners and beginners interested in digital skills
Educators who want to share learning materials

Key Feature :
User registration and login
User Profiles 
Courses and lessons
Progress tracking for learners
Mobile-friendly and responsive web design


Minimum Viable Product (MVP

For the hackathon, the platform will include:
User sign-up and login system
One beginner course (for example, Digital Literacy Basics)
Lesson viewing system
Progress tracking for completed lessons

Technology Stack
Frontend: React.js
Backend: Node.js (Serverless APIs)
Database: MongoDB (Cloud-hosted)
Hosting: Vercel
Version Control: GitHub



Expected Impact

EduBridge Africa aims to improve access to education and digital skills for students across Africa. The platform will encourage independent learning, reduce learning barriers caused by poor internet access, and support youth empowerment through technology and education.

The platform is designed to grow in the future by adding more courses and features.
